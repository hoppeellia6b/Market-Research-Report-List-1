<p><p>The fire-resistant cable market includes various types such as XPLE (cross-linked polyethylene), LSZH (low smoke zero halogen), PVC (polyvinyl chloride), and EPR (ethylene propylene rubber), each offering distinct fire protection characteristics. XPLE provides excellent thermal stability, while LSZH minimizes smoke and toxic emissions during combustion. PVC is cost-effective and widely used, but less effective in fire scenarios. EPR offers flexibility and resilience under heat. Other cable types may include specialized materials catering to specific industrial needs.</p></p>
