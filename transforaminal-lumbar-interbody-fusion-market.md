<p><p>Transforaminal Lumbar Interbody Fusion (TLIF) is a surgical technique utilized to treat spine-related disorders, particularly those affecting the lumbar region. This procedure involves the insertion of an interbody cage filled with bone graft into the disc space to stabilize the spine and promote fusion between adjacent vertebrae. TLIF is preferred for its minimally invasive approach, which leads to reduced recovery times and lower complication rates compared to traditional open surgeries.</p><p>The Transforaminal Lumbar Interbody Fusion Market is witnessing substantial growth fueled by an increasing prevalence of spinal disorders, a surge in the aging population, and advancements in surgical technology. The market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 14.9% during the forecast period. Latest trends include the development of innovative interbody devices, such as patient-specific implants and bioactive materials that enhance bone integration. Furthermore, the rise of robotics and navigation systems in spinal surgery is improving procedural accuracy and outcomes, contributing to a growing demand for TLIF procedures. Additionally, increasing awareness and acceptance of minimally invasive surgical options among patients are further propelling market expansion in the orthopedic and neurosurgical sectors.</p></p>
