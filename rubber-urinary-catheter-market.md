<p><p>The rubber urinary catheter market comprises two primary types: intermittent urinary catheters and indwelling urinary catheters. Intermittent catheters, designed for temporary use, allow patients to drain urine at scheduled intervals, promoting bladder health and reducing infection risk. In contrast, indwelling catheters are inserted for prolonged periods, remaining in place to continuously drain urine. Both types serve critical roles in urinary management for patients with various conditions, influencing their design, materials, and usage within healthcare settings.</p></p>
