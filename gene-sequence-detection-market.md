<p><p>The gene sequence detection market comprises three main types of amplification techniques: Emulsion PCR, Bridge Amplification, and Single-Molecule Detection. Emulsion PCR allows amplification of DNA in tiny droplets, enhancing separation and reducing contamination. Bridge Amplification, typically used in next-generation sequencing, forms distinct clusters on a solid surface, facilitating high-throughput analysis. Single-Molecule Detection targets individual DNA molecules, providing unparalleled sensitivity and precision. Each method offers unique advantages, catering to various applications in genomics and molecular biology.</p></p>
