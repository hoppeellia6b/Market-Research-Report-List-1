<p><p>The potassium fertilizer market includes several key types, each serving distinct agricultural needs. Potassium Chloride is the most widely used due to its high potassium content and cost-effectiveness. Potassium Sulphate provides both potassium and sulfur, beneficial for chlorine-sensitive crops. Potassium Nitrate offers nitrogen along with potassium, promoting growth. Monopotassium Phosphate supplies both phosphorus and potassium, enhancing flower and fruit development. Potassium Carbonate is utilized for specific crops and soil conditions. Other potassium fertilizers include blends and organic options catering to diverse agricultural practices.</p></p>
